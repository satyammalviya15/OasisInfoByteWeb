Tribute to Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and physicist. He is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electrical supply system.

Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, Croatia, which was then part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire. He studied engineering and physics in Graz and Prague before working for several electrical engineering companies in Europe. In 1884, Tesla migrated to the United States and started working for Thomas Edison's company in New York.

Tesla's most significant breakthrough came with his development of the AC induction motor and transformer, which allowed for the efficient transmission of electricity over long distances. His work laid the foundation for the widespread use of electricity in our everyday lives.

Throughout his career, Tesla obtained numerous patents and made remarkable contributions in various fields, including wireless communication, X-ray imaging, and wireless power transmission. He had a visionary mind and proposed ideas that were far ahead of his time.

"The present is theirs; the future, for which I really worked, is mine." - Nikola Tesla
